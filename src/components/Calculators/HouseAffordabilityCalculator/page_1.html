2.3. Key Financial Formulas Employed

The calculator's accuracy depends on standard financial formulas. The two most critical formulas are for the monthly mortgage payment and the Debt-to-Income ratio.

Monthly Payment Formula (Principal & Interest)

This formula calculates the fixed monthly payment required to fully amortize a loan over its term.

M = P × [r(1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n - 1]
  • M = Total monthly mortgage payment (P&I)
  • P = The principal loan amount
  • r = The monthly interest rate (annual rate divided by 12)
  • n = The number of payments over the loan's lifetime (loan term in months)

In this calculator's workflow, this formula is used in reverse (solving for P) to determine the maximum loan amount based on the affordable monthly P&I payment.

Debt-to-Income (DTI) Ratio Formula

DTI is a primary metric used by lenders to assess a borrower's ability to manage monthly payments and repay debts.

DTI = (Total Monthly Debt Payments / Gross Monthly Income) × 100
  • Total Monthly Debt Payments includes the proposed new housing payment (PITI + HOA) plus all other existing monthly debts (car loans, student loans, credit card payments).
  • Gross Monthly Income is the total income before any taxes or deductions.

The calculator uses the DTI as a hard limit (40% in this case) to cap the total monthly payment allowed.

3.4. Debt-to-Income (DTI) Ratio Analysis

The Debt-to-Income ratio is the cornerstone of this affordability calculation. It ensures that the borrower's total debt load remains within a manageable limit defined by the lender. In this scenario, the DTI limit was set to 40%. The calculation works to ensure the final housing payment, when added to existing debts, does not exceed this threshold.

Understanding DTI

Lenders use DTI to measure your ability to manage payments. A lower DTI indicates a good balance between debt and income, posing less risk to the lender. A high DTI can signal that an individual has too much debt for their income level.

  • Front-End DTI (or Housing Ratio): Considers only your housing-related costs (PITI+HOA) against your gross monthly income.
  • Back-End DTI: Considers all your monthly debt payments (housing, auto loans, credit cards, etc.) against your gross monthly income. This is the more comprehensive and commonly used metric.
